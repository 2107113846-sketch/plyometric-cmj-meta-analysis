## Significance and Novelty

This meta-analysis makes three incremental contributions beyond the existing evidence base:

1. **First meta-analysis to adopt CMJ arm position as an explicit inclusion criterion.** Prior meta-analyses (Markovic 2007; Slimani 2016; Moran 2019; Ramirez-Campillo et al. 2020, 2021) did not systematically distinguish between hands-on-hips CMJ and arm-swing CMJ/Abalakov/Vertec protocols, despite evidence that arm swing contributes 10–15% additional jump height (Harman et al. 1990). Our primary analysis pool is restricted to strict hands-on-hips/arms-akimbo CMJ (k = 15 RCTs), with arm-position-undefined (k = 6) and CMJ-with-arm-swing (k = 6) subgroups analyzed separately. This design reduces measurement confounding and provides the purest estimate of plyometric training effects on lower-limb SSC function currently available.

2. **Comprehensive dose-response characterization across three analytical tiers.** We combine categorical subgrouping (short ≤6 wk / medium 7–10 wk / long >10 wk) with continuous meta-regression and multivariate models, revealing a non-linear dose-response pattern: moderate and consistent effects for short-term training (g ≈ +0.71, I² = 13.8%) → gradual increase in the medium term (g ≈ +0.95, I² = 56.4%) → large but highly variable effects in the long term (g ≈ +1.85, I² = 94%). This pattern has direct implications for periodization decisions by coaches and practitioners.

3. **Open-science transparency exceeding field norms.** The analysis is pre-registered on PROSPERO (CRD420261422906), all analysis code and extracted data are publicly archived on Zenodo (https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20748080), one protocol deviation (R19 exclusion due to irreconcilable SD/SE ambiguity) is explicitly acknowledged and verified through sensitivity analysis, and dual AI-use declarations are provided.

## Methodological Highlights

- **Effect size metric**: Hedges' g computed from pre-post change scores (r = 0.7 assumed), with r = 0.5 and r = 0.9 sensitivity analyses
- **Pooling model**: REML random-effects model with Q-profile 95% CIs for I² and prediction intervals
- **Risk of bias**: PEDro scale, dual-independent rating
- **Evidence certainty**: GRADE framework with explicit downgrade justification
- **Publication bias**: Egger regression + Trim-and-Fill + SE-g correlation triangulation
- **Sample**: 29 RCTs, N = 718 participants (intervention n = 367, control n = 351), published 2003–2025

## Relevance to JSCR

Fifteen of the 29 included RCTs and approximately 40% of our reference list are drawn from JSCR, reflecting the journal's central role as the primary publication venue for plyometric training research. Our manuscript directly extends the line of work published by Ramirez-Campillo, Moran, de Villarreal, and others in JSCR, while introducing methodological refinements that we believe raise the standard for future meta-analyses in this domain.
